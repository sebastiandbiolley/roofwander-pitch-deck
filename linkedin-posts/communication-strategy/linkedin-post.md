One thing we realized while building a marketplace:

Communication should follow user actions, not just schedules.

At Roofwander, we try to structure communication around events happening on the platform, rather than sending generic campaigns.

Because most of the time, what a user just did matters more than when the message is sent.

For example:

• someone creates an account
• someone views listings
• someone saves a listing
• someone contacts an owner
• someone completes a booking

Each of these actions can trigger different communication.

Transactional emails

These are messages directly tied to the product:

• booking confirmations
• payment updates
• account-related notifications

They are essential for the marketplace to function smoothly.

Event-based emails

Some emails are triggered by behavior on the platform.

For example:

• onboarding emails after signup
• reminders after saving listings
• follow-ups after certain actions

The goal is to make communication contextual to what the user is doing.

Retargeting

Another part of the journey happens before signup.

Many users explore listings or locations without creating an account.

When consent allows it, we can still reach them through retargeting, based on events captured by our tracking setup.

This helps us stay relevant even before users enter the email lifecycle.

Still refining this system as the marketplace grows.
