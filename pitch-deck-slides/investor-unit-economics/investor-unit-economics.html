Repeat behavior

Owner supports ≈ 20 bookings over lifetime (assumption)

≈ 1 owner supports ~10 renters

That asymmetry explains why owner CAC can be higher than renter CAC in absolute terms. The 2x and 20x figures are assumptions to be validated with marketplace data.

CAC ceiling (unit economics)

Renter CAC max: €72

Owner CAC max: €80

Because:

72 ÷ 2 = 36
Renter CAC per booking
80 ÷ 20 = 4

Actual CAC must remain below this ceiling for the marketplace to scale sustainably.

Key note: CAC allocation shifts depending on the marketplace bottleneck: supply or demand.

Why CAC declines as the marketplace scales

  • More listings improve selection and conversion
  • More reviews increase trust
  • More completed rentals generate better social proof
  • More SEO pages and content increase organic traffic
  • More brand awareness drives direct traffic
  • Referrals become more important over time
  • Ad campaigns become more efficient with better data and targeting

Implication: As liquidity and organic discovery improve, acquisition becomes cheaper and more predictable.
