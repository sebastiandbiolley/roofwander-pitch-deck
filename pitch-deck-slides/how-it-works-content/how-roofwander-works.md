# TL;DR

## Section 0 — TL;DR

Roofwander is a marketplace for rooftop tent travel.

It connects people who own rooftop tents with travelers who want to experience rooftop camping without buying the equipment.

Supply on the platform comes from two sources:

- Private owners renting out tents they already own (typically one or a few listings)
- Professional partners such as outdoor shops, resellers, and rental companies (can list multiple units)

Travelers can search, compare, and book rooftop tents locally for road trips, festivals, and outdoor travel.

Roofwander generates revenue through two main mechanisms:

- Rental commissions on bookings
- Affiliate commissions when travelers later purchase rooftop tents or gear through partner brands

The platform therefore combines:

- Marketplace transactions — peer and professional rentals
- Product discovery — reviews and comparisons (experience + tent), brands and gear through affiliate partnerships
- Growth infrastructure — SEO, tracking, lifecycle communication, and product systems

This page explains how the marketplace, growth strategy, and product infrastructure actually work.

Owners and professional partners create listings with photos, location, price, and availability. After each rental, travelers leave reviews about both the experience and the tent itself. These reviews build a comparison layer for the buy side: when renters later research rooftop tents to purchase, they can compare models based on real-user feedback. Travelers search for nearby rooftop tents, compare listings (including reviews), and send booking requests. Once a booking is confirmed, the transaction is processed securely through the platform.

---

## Provider Journey

For owners and partners, the journey looks like this:

- create an account and connect payouts
- publish one or more listings with photos, price, and availability
- receive and respond to booking requests from the inbox
- manage calendar and pricing over time based on demand
- receive payouts after completed rentals

## Rent-to-Buy Loop

Many travelers who rent rooftop tents later decide to purchase their own equipment.

<div class="doc-flow">
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Traveler rents tent</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Experiences rooftop camping</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Researches & compares<br><small>(reviews, feedback)</small></span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Partner retailer or brand</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item highlight">Affiliate commission</span>
</div>

Rentals create reviews about the experience and the tent, which feed into comparison on the buy side. Travelers who have tried different models can make informed purchase decisions based on real feedback.

This creates a rent-to-buy funnel, where the marketplace generates value from both:

- rental transactions
- equipment purchases discovered through the platform (driven by reviews and comparisons)

See The Insight for a deeper explanation of this loop.

## Why this model works

Roofwander combines three reinforcing mechanisms:

<div class="doc-flow">
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Peer supply</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Professional supply</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Marketplace rentals</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item">Product discovery</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-arrow">↓</span>
  <span class="doc-flow-item highlight">Affiliate commerce</span>
</div>

As more tents are listed, more travelers discover rooftop camping, which increases both bookings and future equipment purchases.

## Infrastructure

The marketplace runs on:

- Backend and APIs for listings, bookings, and payments
- Custom frontend for search, listing pages, and docs
- Stripe for payments and payouts
- Google Tag Manager and analytics tools for tracking and optimisation
